Frequently asked questions



Which toys are good for babies?
Things they can reach for, hold, suck on, shake, make noise with—rattles, large rings, squeeze toys, teething toys, soft dolls, textured balls, and vinyl and board books. Things to listen to—books with nursery rhymes and poems, and recordings of lullabies and simple songs.
What toys are good for baby brain development?
Nursery mobile. Objects dancing above a baby's head while lying in a crib stimulate vision and develop attention span. Mirror. Initially, your baby will be fascinated with the changing face and expressions looking back from the mirror. ... Ring stack. ... Push-pull toys.
What should be baby's first toy?
Hearing: Rattles, toys that squeak, and simple musical instruments like egg shakers. Vision: High contrast toys help with baby's vision development. Look for black-and-white toys to help baby. Touch: Squishy and soft toys, like stuffed animals, can bring baby comfort.
How to choose toys for babies?
Read the label. ... Think LARGE. ... Avoid toys that shoot objects into the air. ... Avoid toys that are loud to prevent damage to your child's hearing. ... Look for stuffed toys that are well made. ... Buy plastic toys that are sturdy. ... Avoid toys with toxic materials that could cause poisoning.
